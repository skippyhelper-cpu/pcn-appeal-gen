2. Defective or Absent Signage and Road Markings

A contravention under code 01 can only be established where valid waiting restrictions are in place and lawfully signed. The yellow line markings at the location must comply strictly with the Traffic Signs Regulations and General Directions 2016 (TSRGD 2016), Schedule 6. Any departure from those requirements — including incorrect line width, missing or obscured time-plate signs, or signs positioned contrary to statutory guidance — renders the restriction unenforceable.

In Herron v The Parking Adjudicator [2010] EWHC 1427 (Admin), the High Court confirmed that defects material to the location of the contravention will invalidate a PCN, though immaterial remote defects may not. I invite the authority to confirm that the markings and signage at the exact location of the alleged contravention are fully compliant. Where any deficiency exists, the PCN cannot be sustained.

3. Procedural Impropriety

The PCN must comply with Section 66 of the Traffic Management Act 2004 and Regulation 3 of the Civil Enforcement of Parking Contraventions (England) General Regulations 2007, including the requirement to state the date of the notice and the date of the alleged contravention. Any omission renders the PCN invalid: see the adjudicator's decision in Westminster City Council (Case No. 207 0143971), where the absence of a date of notice was held to be fatal to the PCN.

5. Legal Framework

The authority's power to issue this PCN derives from Section 66 of the Traffic Management Act 2004. The restriction itself must be authorised by a valid Traffic Regulation Order made under Section 1 or Section 6 of the Road Traffic Regulation Act 1984. The authority is required to demonstrate both that the TRO is lawfully made and in force, and that the signage complies with TSRGD 2016. Failure on either point is a complete answer to the charge.

In light of the above, I respectfully request that the Penalty Charge Notice is cancelled in full.

If you do not accept these representations, please treat this letter as a formal request for the following evidence:

  • All Civil Enforcement Officer (CEO) notes and observations made at the time of the alleged contravention;
  • All photographic evidence taken by the CEO or any CCTV/camera system;
  • The relevant Traffic Regulation Order(s) (TRO) and any amendments or variations;
  • Confirmation that the TRO is valid, properly made and currently in force;
  • A full explanation of how all statutory requirements under the Traffic Management Act 2004 and the Civil Enforcement of Parking Contraventions (England) General Regulations 2007 have been satisfied.

I reserve the right to appeal to the Traffic Penalty Tribunal should representations be rejected, and to rely on any additional grounds that may arise from the evidence disclosed.

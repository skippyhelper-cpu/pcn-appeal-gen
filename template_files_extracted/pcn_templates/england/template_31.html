Having reviewed the circumstances and the evidence available, I believe the Penalty Charge Notice should be cancelled on the following grounds.

1. The Contravention Did Not Occur

{{CIRCUMSTANCES}}

Code 31 is a moving traffic contravention. The contravention of a yellow box junction (Diagram 1043, TSRGD 2016) occurs only where a vehicle enters the box and is prevented from exiting it because the exit is not clear. Where the vehicle entered the box junction with a clear exit that subsequently became blocked due to the actions of other road users, no contravention occurred: see R (on the application of Balbo B&C Auto Accessories) v Secretary of State for Transport [2012] and TPT adjudicator decisions on box junction enforcement.

2. Exit Not Blocked at Point of Entry

The critical question is whether the exit road was clear at the moment the vehicle entered the yellow box, not whether it was clear at a later point. Where the evidence — including CCTV footage — does not clearly show that the exit was blocked at the moment of entry, the contravention is not made out.

3. CCTV Evidence Requirements

Code 31 is generally enforced by CCTV. The CCTV footage must show clearly and unambiguously that the vehicle entered the box when the exit was not clear. Where the footage is of poor quality, does not cover the full sequence of events, or is ambiguous, the contravention cannot be proved to the required standard.

4. Yellow Box Marking Compliance

The yellow box junction markings must comply with Diagram 1043 of TSRGD 2016. Any material deviation — including incorrect dimensions, faded markings or a junction that does not meet the conditions for a yellow box — may render the restriction unenforceable.

5. Procedural Impropriety

Moving traffic PCNs issued by post must comply with Section 66 of the Traffic Management Act 2004 and the Civil Enforcement of Parking Contraventions (England) General Regulations 2007. The authority must have the requisite civil enforcement powers to enforce moving traffic contraventions in this location.

7. Legal Framework

Yellow box junctions are regulated under TSRGD 2016 and enforced under the Traffic Management Act 2004 by authorities with moving traffic enforcement (MTE) powers. The authority must demonstrate it holds the requisite MTE powers, that the box junction is lawfully marked, and that the CCTV evidence conclusively establishes the contravention.

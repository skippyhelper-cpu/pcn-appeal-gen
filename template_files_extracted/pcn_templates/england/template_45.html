1. The Contravention Did Not Occur

{{CIRCUMSTANCES}}

Code 45 applies to vehicles stopped on a designated taxi rank. The contravention does not occur where the vehicle itself is a licensed taxi lawfully using the rank, where the vehicle was stationary only momentarily for a lawful purpose such as dropping off a passenger, or where the rank markings were insufficient to alert a driver to the restriction.

2. Setting Down or Picking Up Passengers

Where a vehicle paused briefly to set down or collect passengers, it was not "stopped" in the sense required by code 45, provided it did not remain stationary for longer than reasonably necessary for that purpose. Adjudicators have recognised that brief stops of this nature do not necessarily constitute a contravention.

3. Taxi Rank Signage and Marking

A taxi rank must be clearly designated by signs complying with TSRGD 2016 and by road markings. Where the rank markings were faded, absent or not clearly visible, or where the associated upright signs were missing or non-compliant, the rank cannot be enforced.

4. Procedural Impropriety

The PCN must comply with Section 66 of the Traffic Management Act 2004 and the Civil Enforcement of Parking Contraventions (England) General Regulations 2007. I request copies of all CEO notes and evidence supporting the contravention, including the time the vehicle was first and last observed.

6. Legal Framework

Taxi ranks are designated by TROs under the Road Traffic Regulation Act 1984 and signed in accordance with TSRGD 2016. The authority must prove that the vehicle was stopped on a lawfully designated, properly signed rank, and that the stop was not for a lawful purpose such as setting down passengers.

In light of the above, I respectfully request that the Penalty Charge Notice is cancelled in full.

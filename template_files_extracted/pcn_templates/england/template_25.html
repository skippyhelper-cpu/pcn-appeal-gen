Having reviewed the circumstances and the evidence available, I believe the Penalty Charge Notice should be cancelled on the following grounds.

1. The Contravention Did Not Occur — Loading Was Taking Place

{{CIRCUMSTANCES}}

A code 25 contravention requires that the vehicle was parked in a loading bay without actively loading or unloading. Loading includes not only the physical movement of goods but also reasonable preparatory activity such as the driver's absence to collect or deliver items. Adjudicators have held that a brief absence from the vehicle for loading purposes does not automatically constitute a failure to load: see TPT guidance on loading bay contraventions.

2. CEO Observation Period

The CEO must observe the vehicle for a sufficient period to be satisfied that no loading activity was taking place or about to take place. An observation period that is too short to permit any reasonable loading activity does not provide adequate grounds for issuing a PCN. I request full details of the CEO's observation log, including the times at which observations were made.

3. Defective Loading Bay Signage

A loading bay must be signed in accordance with TSRGD 2016. The signs must clearly state the hours during which loading is restricted and the class of vehicles permitted to use the bay. Where signs are absent, damaged or non-compliant, the loading restriction is unenforceable.

4. Procedural Impropriety

The PCN must comply with Section 66 of the Traffic Management Act 2004 and the Civil Enforcement of Parking Contraventions (England) General Regulations 2007. Any failure to follow the correct procedure, including the requirement to observe the vehicle for an appropriate period, constitutes a procedural impropriety.

6. Legal Framework

Loading bays are designated by TROs under the Road Traffic Regulation Act 1984 and signed under TSRGD 2016. The authority must prove that the vehicle was parked in the bay during restricted hours without any loading activity taking place, and that the bay was lawfully constituted and properly signed.

In light of the above, I respectfully request that the Penalty Charge Notice is cancelled in full.

If you do not accept these representations, please treat this letter as a formal request for the following evidence:

  • The relevant Traffic Regulation Order(s) (TRO) and any amendments or variations;
  • Confirmation that the TRO is valid, properly made and currently in force;
  • A full explanation of how all statutory requirements under the Traffic Management Act 2004 and the Civil Enforcement of Parking Contraventions (England) General Regulations 2007 have been satisfied.

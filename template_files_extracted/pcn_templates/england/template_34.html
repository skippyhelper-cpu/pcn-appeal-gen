Having reviewed the circumstances and the evidence available, I believe the Penalty Charge Notice should be cancelled on the following grounds.

1. The Contravention Did Not Occur

{{CIRCUMSTANCES}}

Code 34 is enforced where a vehicle is observed in a bus lane during restricted hours. The contravention does not occur where the vehicle was making a lawful permitted turn or entering a lawful loading area, where the bus lane restriction was not in operation at the relevant time, or where the vehicle falls within an exempt class (e.g., licensed taxis, bicycles, or other vehicles permitted by the TRO).

2. Exempt Vehicles

The TRO designating the bus lane will specify which classes of vehicle are exempt from the restriction. This typically includes licensed taxis, motorcycles (in some schemes), and pedal cycles. If the vehicle falls within an exempt class as defined in the relevant TRO, no contravention occurred. I request a copy of the relevant TRO to confirm the exempt classes.

3. Bus Lane Signage and Hours

Bus lane restrictions must be signed in accordance with TSRGD 2016, including clear indication of the hours during which the lane is restricted. Where the restriction was not in force at the time of the alleged contravention, or where signage was absent, obscured or non-compliant, the PCN cannot be sustained.

4. CCTV Evidence Quality

Bus lane contraventions are almost invariably enforced by CCTV. The footage must clearly identify the vehicle, show it in the bus lane during restricted hours, and confirm it is not within an exempt class. Where the footage is ambiguous — for example, where the vehicle is partially in the bus lane at a junction — the contravention may not be made out.

5. Procedural Impropriety

The PCN must be issued and served in accordance with Section 66 of the Traffic Management Act 2004 and the Civil Enforcement of Parking Contraventions (England) General Regulations 2007. Any failure in service, notice content or timing renders the PCN invalid.

7. Legal Framework

Bus lanes are designated by TROs under the Road Traffic Regulation Act 1984 and signed under TSRGD 2016. The authority must demonstrate a valid TRO, compliant signage, and conclusive CCTV evidence establishing that the vehicle — not being within an exempt class — was in the bus lane during restricted hours.

In light of the above, I respectfully request that the Penalty Charge Notice is cancelled in full.

If you do not accept these representations, please treat this letter as a formal request for the following evidence:

  • The relevant Traffic Regulation Order(s) (TRO) and any amendments or variations;
  • Confirmation that the TRO is valid, properly made and currently in force;
  • A full explanation of how all statutory requirements under the Traffic Management Act 2004 and the Civil Enforcement of Parking Contraventions (England) General Regulations 2007 have been satisfied.

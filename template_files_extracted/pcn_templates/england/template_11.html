1. The Contravention Did Not Occur — Payment Was Made

{{CIRCUMSTANCES}}

Code 11 applies to parking places that operate on a cashless or pay-by-phone basis only. If payment was made by any accepted electronic means and a valid session was active at the time of the CEO's observations, no contravention occurred. The authority must demonstrate that it checked payment records before issuing the PCN. Adjudicators have consistently held that where a valid electronic payment session was in place, the PCN cannot be sustained.

2. Failure to Check Payment Records

A CEO issuing a code 11 PCN in a cashless parking place bears a procedural obligation to verify, or to have reasonable grounds to believe, that no payment had been made before issuing the notice. Where the authority failed to check real-time payment records — including pay-by-phone records — before issuing the PCN, this constitutes a procedural impropriety under the Traffic Management Act 2004 and the Civil Enforcement of Parking Contraventions (England) General Regulations 2007.

3. Inadequate Signage for Cashless Parking

The signs indicating that only electronic payment is accepted, and the method(s) by which payment may be made, must comply with TSRGD 2016 and be clearly visible. Where the motorist could not reasonably have been aware that cash was not accepted, or that a specific payment method was required, the contravention cannot be regarded as having occurred.

5. Legal Framework

The authority's power derives from Section 66 of the Traffic Management Act 2004 and the relevant TRO. The Civil Enforcement of Parking Contraventions (England) General Regulations 2007 govern procedural requirements. The burden rests with the authority to prove the contravention occurred.

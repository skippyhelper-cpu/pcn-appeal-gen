2. Payment Was Made and/or Valid

A code 05 contravention requires that the paid-for time had expired at the precise moment the CEO made observations sufficient to issue the PCN. If payment was active at that point — whether by pay and display ticket, electronic payment, or pay-by-phone — no contravention occurred. I note that under adjudicator decisions including Apcoa Parking v Driver (TPT, various), authorities must demonstrate that they have checked payment records with reasonable thoroughness before issuing a PCN for a pay-by-phone or electronic payment parking place. Any failure to do so constitutes a procedural irregularity.

3. Defective Payment Equipment

Where a pay and display machine was out of order or malfunctioning at the relevant time, the motorist cannot be penalised for an inability to pay. The authority should confirm the operational status of any machine at the location at the time of the alleged contravention. A failure to maintain operational equipment may amount to a procedural impropriety under the Traffic Management Act 2004.

4. Observation Period

Adjudicators have held that the CEO must be present for a sufficient period to observe the contravention. Where the PCN was issued before the paid time had actually expired (accounting for any grace period applicable under the authority's policy), the contravention did not occur.

6. Legal Framework

The authority's power to enforce paid parking derives from Section 66 of the Traffic Management Act 2004 and the relevant Traffic Regulation Order. The Civil Enforcement of Parking Contraventions (England) General Regulations 2007 govern procedural requirements. Any departure from those requirements may render the PCN invalid.

In light of the above, I respectfully request that the Penalty Charge Notice is cancelled in full.

  • A full explanation of how all statutory requirements under the Traffic Management Act 2004 and the Civil Enforcement of Parking Contraventions (England) General Regulations 2007 have been satisfied.

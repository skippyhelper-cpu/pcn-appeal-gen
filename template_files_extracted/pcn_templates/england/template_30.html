Having reviewed the circumstances and the evidence available, I believe the Penalty Charge Notice should be cancelled on the following grounds.

1. The Contravention Did Not Occur — The Vehicle Was Within the Permitted Period

{{CIRCUMSTANCES}}

Code 30 requires that the maximum permitted parking duration was exceeded at the time the CEO made observations sufficient to justify issuing the PCN. If the vehicle arrived after the time recorded by the CEO, or if the return time was within the permitted period, no contravention occurred. I request full details of the CEO's arrival and observation times.

2. Observation and Grace Period

Many authorities operate a grace period policy at the end of a permitted parking period. Adjudicators at the TPT have held that where a vehicle is returned or removed within a reasonable grace period, the PCN should be cancelled. I invite the authority to confirm whether a grace period applies and whether it was observed in this case.

3. Time Plate Signage Compliance

The permitted duration must be clearly indicated by time-plate signs complying with TSRGD 2016. Where the time restriction sign was absent, obscured or non-compliant, the time limit cannot be enforced. Any deficiency in signage at the exact location of the contravention is a complete defence.

4. Procedural Impropriety

The PCN must comply with Section 66 of the Traffic Management Act 2004 and the Civil Enforcement of Parking Contraventions (England) General Regulations 2007. I request confirmation that the CEO's notes accurately record the times of first and subsequent observations and the basis on which the contravention was believed to have occurred.

6. Legal Framework

Time-limited parking is authorised by TROs under the Road Traffic Regulation Act 1984 and signed in accordance with TSRGD 2016. The authority must demonstrate that the vehicle exceeded the permitted period and that the restriction was lawfully constituted and properly signed.

In light of the above, I respectfully request that the Penalty Charge Notice is cancelled in full.

If you do not accept these representations, please treat this letter as a formal request for the following evidence:

  • All Civil Enforcement Officer (CEO) notes and observations made at the time of the alleged contravention;
  • All photographic evidence taken by the CEO or any CCTV/camera system;
  • The relevant Traffic Regulation Order(s) (TRO) and any amendments or variations;
  • Confirmation that the TRO is valid, properly made and currently in force;
  • A full explanation of how all statutory requirements under the Traffic Management Act 2004 and the Civil Enforcement of Parking Contraventions (England) General Regulations 2007 have been satisfied.

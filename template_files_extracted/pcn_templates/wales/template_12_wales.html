1. The Contravention Did Not Occur — A Valid Permit Was Displayed

{{CIRCUMSTANCES}}

Code 12 requires the vehicle to have been parked without a valid permit, voucher or pay and display ticket. A CEO must take reasonable steps to examine any permit before issuing a PCN. If any valid authority to park was present, the contravention did not occur.

2. Virtual Permit Checking

Where a virtual permit scheme operates, the authority must check its database before issuing a code 12 PCN. Failure to do so is a procedural impropriety under the Traffic Management Act 2004 and the Civil Enforcement of Parking Contraventions (Wales) Regulations 2008.

3. Defective Bay Markings or Signage

A residents' or shared use bay must be designated by a valid TRO and signed in accordance with TSRGD 2016. Where markings or signs are non-compliant, the bay designation may be unenforceable.

5. Legal Framework

The authority must demonstrate a valid TRO, compliant signage and the absence of any valid authority to park. In Wales, the Civil Enforcement of Parking Contraventions (Wales) Regulations 2008 and relevant Welsh Government guidance also apply.

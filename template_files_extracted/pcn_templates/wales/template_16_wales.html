1. The Contravention Did Not Occur — A Valid Permit Was Held and/or Displayed

{{CIRCUMSTANCES}}

Code 16 applies to permit-only bays or zones. If a valid permit was displayed or a virtual permit was active, no contravention occurred. The authority must have checked virtual permit records before issuing the PCN.

2. Virtual Permits

Where the scheme operates on a virtual basis, no physical display is required. A PCN issued for failure to display in a virtual permit zone is a procedural impropriety under the Traffic Management Act 2004 and the Civil Enforcement of Parking Contraventions (Wales) Regulations 2008.

3. Zone Boundary and Signage

The permit zone must be clearly marked by entry signs complying with TSRGD 2016. Where zone signage was ambiguous or non-compliant, it cannot be established that the vehicle was within the controlled zone.

4. Mitigating Circumstances

{{MITIGATING_DETAILS}}

5. Legal Framework

Permit zones in Wales must be established by a valid TRO and signed in accordance with TSRGD 2016. The authority must comply with the Civil Enforcement of Parking Contraventions (Wales) Regulations 2008 and any relevant Welsh Government guidance.

In light of the above, I respectfully request that the Penalty Charge Notice is cancelled in full.

If you do not accept these representations, please treat this letter as a formal request for the following evidence:

  • All Civil Enforcement Officer (CEO) notes and observations made at the time of the alleged contravention;
  • All photographic evidence taken by the CEO or any CCTV/camera system;
  • The relevant Traffic Regulation Order(s) (TRO) and any amendments or variations;
  • Confirmation that the TRO is valid, properly made and currently in force;
  • A full explanation of how all statutory requirements under the Traffic Management Act 2004 and the Civil Enforcement of Parking Contraventions (Wales) Regulations 2008 have been satisfied.

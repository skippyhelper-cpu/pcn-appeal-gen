Having reviewed the circumstances and the evidence available, I believe the Penalty Charge Notice should be cancelled on the following grounds.

1. The Contravention Did Not Occur — A Valid Blue Badge Was Displayed

{{CIRCUMSTANCES}}

A CEO must make reasonable efforts to inspect any displayed Blue Badge before issuing a PCN. Where the badge was present but obscured, the CEO must take all practicable steps to read it. A failure to do so constitutes procedural impropriety.

2. Badge Was Valid at Time of Issue

The contravention does not occur if the badge was valid at the time of the alleged contravention. The authority must confirm the precise expiry date of the badge and exact time of the contravention.

3. Disabled Bay Designation Compliance

A designated disabled bay must be established by a valid TRO and signed in accordance with TSRGD 2016. Where the bay marking or sign is non-compliant, the designation may be unenforceable.

4. Mitigating Circumstances

{{MITIGATING_DETAILS}}

5. Legal Framework

In Wales, the Blue Badge scheme is governed by Section 21 of the Chronically Sick and Disabled Persons Act 1970 and the Disabled Persons (Badges for Motor Vehicles) (Wales) Regulations 2000. The Civil Enforcement of Parking Contraventions (Wales) Regulations 2008 also apply. The authority must demonstrate a valid TRO, compliant signage, and the absence of a valid badge displayed in the prescribed manner.

In light of the above, I respectfully request that the Penalty Charge Notice is cancelled in full.

If you do not accept these representations, please treat this letter as a formal request for the following evidence:

  • All Civil Enforcement Officer (CEO) notes and observations made at the time of the alleged contravention;
  • All photographic evidence taken by the CEO or any CCTV/camera system;
  • The relevant Traffic Regulation Order(s) (TRO) and any amendments or variations;
  • Confirmation that the TRO is valid, properly made and currently in force;
  • A full explanation of how all statutory requirements under the Traffic Management Act 2004 and the Civil Enforcement of Parking Contraventions (Wales) Regulations 2008 have been satisfied.

I reserve the right to appeal to the Traffic Penalty Tribunal should representations be rejected, and to rely on any additional grounds that may arise from the evidence disclosed.

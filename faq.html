Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about PCN appeals

Council PCNs are issued by local authorities and are legally enforceable under the Traffic Management Act 2004. They typically:

  • Have "Penalty Charge Notice" or "PCN" written on them
  • Are issued by your local council
  • Have fines of £60-£130 (reduced by 50% if paid within 14 days)
  • Can be appealed to the council and then to the Traffic Penalty Tribunal

Private parking tickets are issued by private companies and are not legally enforceable in the same way. They:

  • May be called "Parking Charge Notices" (different from PCNs)
  • Are issued by private parking companies
  • Are invoices for alleged breach of contract
  • Require different appeal processes

Our service handles council PCNs only.

You have different timeframes depending on the stage:

  • 14 days: Pay at reduced rate (usually 50% discount)
  • 28 days: Make an informal appeal (before the Notice to Owner is issued)
  • 28 days from Notice to Owner: Make formal representations
  • After formal rejection: Appeal to Traffic Penalty Tribunal within 28 days

Important: If you appeal within 14 days and your appeal is rejected, you usually still get the 14-day discount period to pay.

Success rates vary, but many appeals are successful. Common winning grounds include:

  • Signs or road markings were unclear, obscured, or missing
  • The contravention didn't actually occur
  • You were loading/unloading (allowed activity)
  • You had broken down
  • You had a valid permit but it wasn't displayed
  • The council made a procedural error
  • Mitigating circumstances (emergency, medical issue)

Even if you think you have no chance, it's often worth appealing as councils sometimes don't contest appeals or make errors that work in your favour.

Useful evidence includes:

  • Photographs: Signs, road markings, your vehicle position, surrounding area
  • Receipts: Parking payment receipts, breakdown service receipts
  • Permit evidence: Photos of valid permit, permit application confirmation
  • Medical evidence: If appealing on medical grounds
  • Witness statements: From passengers or bystanders
  • Weather reports: If weather affected signage visibility

Tip: Take photos as soon as possible after receiving the ticket, before conditions change.

If the council rejects your appeal, you have options:

  1. Pay the fine: You can pay at any stage. If you pay within 14 days of the rejection, you may still get the discount.
  2. Appeal to the Traffic Penalty Tribunal: This is an independent body that reviews council decisions. It's free and you don't need a lawyer.

Important: If you don't pay or appeal to the tribunal within the deadline, the council can increase the fine and eventually take enforcement action.
